# How SmartMouth Works

## The Problem: Volatile Sulfur Compounds (VSCs)

Bad breath is caused by anaerobic bacteria in the mouth consuming protein and producing **volatile sulfur compounds (VSCs)** — specifically hydrogen sulfide and methyl mercaptan — as a byproduct. These sulfur gases are what smell.

Most mouthwashes mask VSCs with strong flavors (mint, menthol). The odor returns in 20–30 minutes.

## The Solution: Zinc Ion Activation

**Developer:** Dr. Israel Kleinberg, Chairman of Oral Biology and Pathology, SUNY Stony Brook

SmartMouth's patented zinc ions **interrupt the bacterial consumption process** before sulfur gas is produced:

```
Normal process:  Bacteria + Protein → Sulfur Gas (VSCs) → Bad Breath
SmartMouth:      Zinc Ions block bacteria → No sulfur produced → No bad breath
```

The zinc ions don't kill bacteria (like chlorhexidine does, which causes staining) — they specifically inhibit the sulfur-producing metabolic pathway. This is more targeted and produces fewer side effects.

## The Dual-Chamber Delivery System

SmartMouth's technology requires **two separate solutions** that must combine fresh at point of use:
- **Chamber 1:** Zinc ion solution
- **Chamber 2:** Activation solution

When combined, the zinc ions "activate" — this is why the mouthwash turns **green** at the moment of mixing, and gradually shifts to **blue** as the zinc ions enter their protective state.
