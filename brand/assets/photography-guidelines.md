# Photography & Visual Direction

## Product Photography

**Style:** Studio-lit, white/neutral background, minimal shadows
**Focus:** Full product lineup visibility — show the dual-chamber bottle clearly
**Angle:** Slight 3/4 angle to show dual chambers; flat lay for catalog use
**Lighting:** Soft, even — avoid harsh shadows; clinical cleanliness
**Editing:** Minimal post-processing; accurate color representation

### Hero Shots
- Show the mouthwash liquid color change: green (activated) → blue (post-activation)
- This is a unique brand visual — USE IT. It's story, not just product.
- Dual-chamber bottle is the hero object — competitor bottles look generic by comparison

## Lifestyle Photography

**Style:** Confident people in real-world moments
**Settings:** Professional office, social gatherings, outdoor morning routines
**Mood:** Calm confidence — not relief (they don't have bad breath) — proactive and assured
**Diversity:** Range of ages 28–60, professional contexts
**Avoid:** Comedy, embarrassment, fear-based scenarios, close-up mouth/teeth stereotypes

### Do's
- Morning routine — person confident and ready for the day
- Professional context — presentation, handshake, conversation
- Intimate moment — talking closely, confident
- Clean, bright home environments

### Don'ts
- Cartoon bad breath clouds
- Disgusted reactions from others
- "Before/after" shame-based framing
- Generic stock photo feel

## Clinical/Scientific Imagery

**Use for:** How It Works section, ingredient explanations, clinical study references
**Style:** Lab settings, microscopy aesthetic, diagram illustrations
**Colors:** White + green + blue (matching brand palette)
**Elements:** Molecule diagrams, zinc ion visualization, dual-chamber exploded view

## Color Treatment in Photography

- Keep backgrounds white or very light neutral
- Allow the product's green/blue colors to be the accent
- Avoid warm-toned or golden photography — that's spa/beauty, not clinical oral care
- High contrast preferred — sharp, clean, credible
